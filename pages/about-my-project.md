problem: |
 Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) affects millions of people worldwide, yet choosing an antidepressant is still largely a trial-and-   error process. Patients often spend weeks taking a medication before doctors can determine whether it is helping, and many people    must repeat this process several times before finding an effective treatment. This delay prolongs suffering and makes recovery more  difficult.

 Researchers know that biological factors such as genetics, gene activity, and epigenetic changes influence treatment response, but   most existing prediction methods analyze these signals separately or treat them as simple lists of numbers. These approaches         struggle to capture the complex relationships between different biological systems and often perform poorly because psychiatric      datasets contain many more features than patient samples. This project addresses that gap by developing a method that combines       multiple layers of biological information and incorporates knowledge learned from biomedical language models to improve prediction   of antidepressant response.
