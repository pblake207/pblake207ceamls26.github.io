what_i_learned: |
Today, we attended the AWS Summit in Washington, D.C., and it was easily one of the most informative events I have attended all year. The experience showed me just how much there is to learn in the fields of AI, cybersecurity, and cloud computing. It also made me realize that while I have learned a lot through this program, the gap between what I currently know and the breadth of knowledge in the industry is much larger than I imagined. Rather than feeling discouraged, it motivated me to continue learning and exploring new areas.

One of the highlights of the day was connecting with professionals and leaders working at the intersection of cybersecurity, AI, and defense. Listening to their conversations gave me insight into the technologies, challenges, and priorities that are shaping the future of the field. It was exciting to hear about topics like agentic AI, threat detection, cloud security, and the role AI is beginning to play in national defense.

I also had the opportunity to visit different company booths, ask questions, and learn about career paths that I had not previously considered. The summit gave me a much clearer picture of where my interests might fit within the technology industry and introduced me to ideas that I want to explore further.
 
blockers: |
 The biggest challenge today was simply the amount of walking and networking. By the end of the summit, my social battery was definitely running low. Even so, I pushed myself to continue having conversations and making the most of the opportunity.
 
reflection: |
 Overall, today was an amazing experience, and I like to think I took full advantage of it. I left the summit with new ideas, new connections, and a much better understanding of where the industry is headed. It also gave me a lot to think about as I continue exploring potential career paths in AI, cybersecurity, and defense. I am grateful for the opportunity to attend and know it will be one of the most memorable experiences from this program.
