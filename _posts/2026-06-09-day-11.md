what_i_learned: |

 Today, we met as a group and continued cleaning our dataset. While data cleaning can sometimes feel repetitive, I am       becoming more comfortable with the process and understanding why it is such an important part of research. It was helpful  to get more hands-on experience working with real data and preparing it for analysis.

 We also had a lesson on conducting literature reviews. I learned more about how researchers find, evaluate, and            synthesize existing studies to identify gaps in the literature and support their own research questions. This will be      useful as our group continues refining our project.

 After lunch, we met with our graduate mentor and were joined by our faculty mentor. After reviewing our design document,   they realized that our original research direction focusing on Type 1 Diabetes was not well aligned with the conference    we are hoping to publish in. As a result, our group will be pivoting our project to focus on depression and anxiety        instead. Although this was unexpected, it was helpful to receive feedback early enough to make adjustments before getting  too far into the project.


blockers: |
 I did not encounter any major blockers today. Most of my challenges involved understanding the implications of our         project pivot, but discussions with my mentors helped clarify why the change was necessary.

reflection: |
 Today was definitely surprising. While I understand why we are changing directions, it is a little frustrating to feel     like we have to rethink parts of our project after spending time developing our original approach. At the same time, I     know this is part of the research process, and it is better to make adjustments now than later. Moving forward, I am       curious to see what opportunities this new focus on depression and anxiety will create and how our project will evolve.
